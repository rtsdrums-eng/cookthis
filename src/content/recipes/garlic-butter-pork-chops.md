## Ingredients

- 4 bone-in pork chops (about 1 inch thick)
- 3 tablespoons butter
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

## Instructions

1. **Season the chops:** Pat pork chops dry. Season both sides generously with salt, pepper, paprika, and thyme.

2. **Sear:** Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear pork chops for 4-5 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through (145°F internal).

3. **Baste with butter:** Reduce heat to medium-low. Add butter and garlic to the skillet. Once melted, tilt the pan and spoon the garlic butter over the chops for about 1 minute.

4. **Rest and serve:** Transfer to a plate and let rest 3 minutes. Spoon pan juices over the top and garnish with parsley.

## Notes

- **Storage:** Leftovers keep in the fridge for 3 days. Reheat gently to avoid drying out.
- **Variations:** Works great with boneless chops too — just reduce cook time by 1-2 minutes per side.
- **Tips:** Let chops come to room temperature for 15 minutes before cooking for more even results.
