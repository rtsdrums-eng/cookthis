## Ingredients

- 8 thick slices brioche or challah bread
- 4 large eggs
- ¾ cup whole milk
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 teaspoon cinnamon
- 2 tablespoons butter, for cooking
- Maple syrup, for serving
- Powdered sugar, for dusting
- Fresh berries, for serving

## Instructions

1. **Make the custard:** Whisk together eggs, milk, vanilla, and cinnamon in a shallow dish.

2. **Soak the bread:** Dip each bread slice into the custard, letting it soak for about 15 seconds per side. Don't let it get soggy — just a good coating.

3. **Cook:** Melt butter in a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Cook bread for 2-3 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through.

4. **Serve:** Stack on plates. Dust with powdered sugar, top with fresh berries, and drizzle with maple syrup.

## Notes

- **Storage:** Cooked French toast freezes well. Reheat in the toaster for a quick weekday breakfast.
- **Variations:** Use cinnamon-swirl bread for extra flavor. Add a tablespoon of orange zest to the custard for a citrusy twist.
- **Tips:** Day-old bread works best — it's drier and soaks up the custard without falling apart.
