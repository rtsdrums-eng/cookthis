## Ingredients

- 1 lb ground beef
- 1 small onion, diced
- 1 green bell pepper, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- ¾ cup ketchup
- 2 tablespoons brown sugar
- 1 tablespoon yellow mustard
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 4 hamburger buns, toasted

## Instructions

1. **Brown the beef:** Cook ground beef in a large skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up as it cooks, about 5 minutes. Drain excess fat.

2. **Soften the veggies:** Add onion and bell pepper. Cook for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds more.

3. **Build the sauce:** Stir in ketchup, brown sugar, mustard, Worcestershire, and vinegar. Bring to a simmer and cook for 8-10 minutes until thickened.

4. **Serve:** Season with salt and pepper. Pile onto toasted buns.

## Notes

- **Storage:** The meat mixture keeps in the fridge for 4 days or freezes for up to 3 months.
- **Variations:** Use ground turkey for a lighter version. Add a pinch of cayenne for heat.
- **Tips:** Toast the buns in butter — it holds up better against the saucy filling.
- **Make Ahead:** Make the meat mixture ahead and reheat when ready to serve.
