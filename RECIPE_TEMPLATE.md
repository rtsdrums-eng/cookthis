## Content Guidelines

### Tone & Style
- **Conversational but concise** - Like texting a friend a recipe
- **No fluff** - Get straight to the point
- **Practical** - Focus on what home cooks actually need to know
- **Encouraging** - Make cooking feel achievable

### Recipe Selection Criteria
- **Common ingredients** - Things people likely have or can easily find
- **Reasonable time** - Most recipes under 45 minutes total
- **Tested techniques** - Standard home kitchen methods
- **Couple/family friendly** - 2-6 servings typically

### Tag Guidelines
Choose 4-6 relevant tags from:
- **Speed:** quick, 30-minute, 15-minute, weeknight, make-ahead
- **Dietary:** vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, dairy-free, keto, paleo
- **Meal type:** breakfast, brunch, lunch, dinner, snack, dessert, appetizer
- **Cuisine:** italian, mexican, asian, mediterranean, american, indian
- **Protein:** chicken, beef, pork, seafood, shrimp, salmon, tofu, eggs
- **Season:** summer, fall, winter, spring, holiday
- **Cooking method:** one-pan, sheet-pan, slow-cooker, instant-pot, no-cook, grilled
- **Occasion:** date-night, party, potluck, meal-prep, comfort-food, healthy

### Categories for Distribution
When generating multiple recipes, aim for this distribution:
- **Dinner:** 40% (quick weeknight meals, one-pan dishes, date night specials)
- **Breakfast:** 20% (quick morning meals, weekend brunch)
- **Lunch:** 15% (sandwiches, salads, bowls)
- **Sides:** 15% (vegetables, starches, salads)
- **Snacks/Apps:** 5% (party food, healthy snacks)
- **Desserts:** 5% (simple sweets, no-bake options)

## Example Recipe (Correct Format)

```markdown
---
title: "Honey Garlic Salmon Bowl"
description: "A perfectly balanced weeknight dinner with flaky salmon, crisp vegetables, and an addictive honey garlic glaze."
author: "Ryan"
prepTime: 10
cookTime: 15
totalTime: 25
servings: 2
category: "dinner"
tags: ["salmon", "seafood", "30-minute", "healthy", "date-night", "gluten-free"]
image: "/images/honey-garlic-salmon-bowl.jpg"
imageAlt: "Glazed salmon over rice with colorful vegetables"
date: 2026-02-22
draft: false
---

## Ingredients

### For the Salmon
- 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
- 2 tablespoons honey
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- Salt and pepper to taste

### For the Bowl
- 1½ cups cooked jasmine rice
- 1 cup broccoli florets
- 1 cup snap peas
- 1 carrot, julienned
- 2 green onions, sliced
- 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

## Instructions

1. **Mix the glaze:** In a small bowl, whisk together honey, soy sauce, garlic, and ginger. Set aside.

2. **Cook the salmon:** Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Season salmon with salt and pepper. Cook skin-side up for 4 minutes until golden.

3. **Add the glaze:** Flip salmon and pour half the glaze over it. Cook 3-4 minutes more, spooning glaze over the fish as it thickens.

4. **Steam the vegetables:** In the last 5 minutes of cooking, add broccoli and snap peas to a steamer basket over boiling water. Steam until tender-crisp.

5. **Assemble the bowls:** Divide rice between two bowls. Top with salmon, steamed vegetables, and fresh carrots. Drizzle with remaining glaze and sprinkle with green onions and sesame seeds.

## Notes

- **Storage:** Keep leftover components separate in the fridge for up to 3 days.
- **Variations:** Try with chicken thighs or tofu instead of salmon.
- **Tips:** Don't overcook the vegetables - they should still have a slight crunch.
- **Make Ahead:** The glaze can be made 3 days in advance.
```

4. **Skill levels** - Most should be beginner-friendly with a few intermediate
5. **Equipment** - Assume basic kitchen tools, note when special equipment needed
